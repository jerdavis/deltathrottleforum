Placed orders for my parts last night and have been trying to figure out how to handle the 3d printing. All of the online services and even the local UPS store that has a printer all come in around $300. Ouch! I'm a member of the local maker space, and someone has recently taken over ownership of the 3d printing there. He's working on getting the printers up and running again and calibrated. I've offered to help out were I can. So hopefully I can get in there this week and perhaps start working on the prints.

I picked up some offcuts at the local metal supply over the weekend to play around and practice. I've been trying to take some measurements off the STL files. I'm curious if you could easily upload dimensional drawings of some of the parts for those who might want to try to make one with other fabrication means?

Looking forward to putting this thing together. Thanks for sharing!

After picking up a better end mill I was able to get going last night and milled a handle base from 1/4" aluminum plate. Still need to drill the screw holes to mount the handle once it's printed and drill and tap holes to mount the pivot linkages. Working up a drawing for the base plate and hoping to cut it this weekend. I'll likely still print the pivot arm pieces and handle. I haven't figured out an easy way to slot an aluminum rod for the D on the potentiometer shaft and can't use set screws if they'd be covered up with the spring, so I'd need to redesign the part and probably order different springs.

Re: Starting build soon

by jeruw » Tue Mar 03, 2015 4:34 pm

Thanks for that. It looks like it's missing measurements for placement of the uprights though? I'll need to look over it again. I cut my base last night and the measurements are looking good compared to what I took from the STL model. I'm redesigning the uprights to have more of "wing" shape. I figured I could get away with that with the aluminum. I'll cut both sides out of 1/4" plate. I was going to weld them to the base but given my relative inexperience tig welding aluminum I think I'll just bolt them down. I'll drill through the base and drill+tap the bottom of the uprights. They'll slot into the slots I cut in the base plate and bolt down. That way I can also eliminate the cut on the right side upright and still be able to mount the pivot arms easily.

I'll post a pic of the base plate later this afternoon.
